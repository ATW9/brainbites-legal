Privacy Policy

Last updated: 15 July 2026

BrainBites (“we”, “us”, “our”) produces short educational videos about cybersecurity, online scams and everyday technology, and publishes them to our own social media channels. This policy explains what information our software handles and how we treat it.

1. Who this policy is for

BrainBites is an automated publishing tool operated by us for our own accounts. It creates videos and posts them to social platforms we own and control (such as YouTube, TikTok, Instagram and Facebook). This policy covers the operation of that tool and this website.

2. Information we access

  • Our own connected accounts. With our authorisation, our software uses official platform APIs to publish videos to our own channels and to read aggregate performance metrics for our own posts (for example view counts, likes and comments).
  • Access tokens. We store the authorisation tokens needed to publish to our own accounts. These are kept privately on our own server and are never shared.
  • No audience data. We do not collect, store, buy, or process personal information about viewers, followers or any other individuals.

3. How we use information

We use the access described above solely to (a) publish our own educational content and (b) understand how our own posts perform in aggregate, so we can improve future content. We do not use it for advertising, profiling, or any decision affecting individuals.

4. Sharing and third parties

We do not sell or share personal data. Video scripts and visuals are generated with the help of a third-party AI service (Anthropic); no personal data about viewers is sent to it. Our content is distributed through third-party platforms (YouTube, TikTok, Meta), each governed by its own privacy policy.

5. Data retention

We retain records of our own published posts and their aggregate metrics for as long as the channel operates. We do not retain any viewer personal information because we do not collect it.

6. Cookies and tracking

This website does not use tracking or advertising cookies.

7. Security

We take reasonable technical measures to protect the credentials and data our software uses, including restricting access and storing tokens privately.

8. Children

Our content is general-audience educational material and is not directed at children under 13.

9. Your rights and contact

If you believe we hold information relating to you, or have any question about this policy, contact us at alex@zanderwilliams.co.uk and we will respond promptly.

10. Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. The “last updated” date above reflects the current version.
